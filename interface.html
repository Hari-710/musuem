LIST OF TOP 10 MUSEUM IN INDIA

  1. Natioanl Musuem, New Delhi

    2. This Musuem is located in heart of the capital, it is one of the largest musuem in India. This musuem has an extensive collection of artifacts from across the India, including relics from the Indus valley civilization, Mughal-era paintings, and ancient manuscripts.This musuem has nearly 17,000 paintings, representing all important styles of Indian miniatures.

    Location: Janpath, New Delhi, India

    Plan Your Visit:

    Timing: Tue-Sun(10:00 AM to 6:00 PM)

    Closed on Mondays and National Holidays

    Entry Fee: Adults: 50 Rupees

    Foreign Nationals: 500 Rupees

    Students up to class 12th: Free entry(With Id-cards)





  2. Indian Musuem, Kolkata

    3. This musuem is the earliest and the largest multipurpose Museum not only in the Indian subcontinent but also in the Asia-pacific region of the world founded in 1814. It has rare collections of antiques, armour and ornaments, fossils, skeletons, mummies and Mughal paintings.It has six sections comprising of thirty fivr galleries of cultural and scientific artifacts.

    Museum type: Museum

    Location: 27, Jawaharlal Nehru Rd, Fire Brigade Head Quarter, New Market Area, Dharmatala, Taltala, Kolkata, West Bengal 700016

    Plan Your Visit:

    Timing: Tue-Sun(10:00 AM to 6:30 PM)

    Closed on Mondays and National Holidays

    Entry Fee: Adults: 50 Rupees

    Foreign Nationals: 500 Rupees

    Students up to class 12th: 20 Rupees





  3. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya, Mumbai

    4. It is located in the heart of South Mumbai near the Gateway of India.Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya, (CSMVS) formerly named the Prince of Wales Museum of Western India, is a museum in Mumbai (Bombay) which documents the history of India from prehistoric to modern times.It was founded during British rule of India in the early years of the 20th century.The museum houses approximately 50,000 exhibits of ancient Indian history as well as objects from foreign lands, categorised primarily into three sections: Art, Archaeology and Natural History

    Museum type: Archaeology, Art and Natural history museum

    Location: Mahatma Gandhi Road, Fort, Mumbai, India

    Plan Your Visit:

    Timing: Mon-Sun(10:15 AM to 6:00 PM)

    Closed on National Holidays

    Entry Fee: Adults above 15 years: 150 Rupees

    College Students(with valid ID-card) 75 Rupees and for School Students(with valid ID-card) 35 Rupees

    Foreign Nationals: Adults above 15 years 700 Rupees and for Childrens 200 Rupees

    Foreign Nationals: Children: 200 Rupees





  4. Salar Jung Museum, Hyderabad

    5. The Salar Jung Museum, one of India's three National Museums, is famous for its eclectic collection gathered by Mir Yousuf Ali Khan, also known as Salar Jung III. The museum features a vast array of exhibits, including Indian art, European paintings, Middle Eastern antiques, and Far Eastern artifacts.

    Museum type: Art Museum

    Location: Dar-ul-Shifa, Hyderabad, Telangana, India

    Plan Your Visit:

    Timing: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM

    Closed on Fidays and National Holidays

    Entry Fee: Adults above 18 years: 50 Rupees and for childrens above 5 years: 20 Rupees

    Foreign Nationals: 500 Rupees per person





  5. Victoria Memorial, Kolkata

    6. A stunning example of colonial architecture, the Victoria Memorial is dedicated to Queen Victoria. The museum within showcases an extensive collection of British colonial artifacts, paintings, and manuscripts, offering a glimpse into the colonial history of India.

    Museum type: Museum

    Location: 1, Queens Way, Maidan, Kolkata, West Bengal 700071

    Plan Your Visit:

    Timing: 5:30 AM to 6:00 PM

    Closed on Mondays and National Holidays

    Entry Fee: 20 Rupees per head

    Foreign Nationals: 200 Rupees per head





  6. Government Museum, Chennai

    7. This institution is renowned for its impressive collections in archaeology, numismatics, and natural history. The Bronze Gallery, featuring exquisite Chola bronzes, is a highlight.

    Museum type: Art museum, History museum

    Location: Egmore, Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India

    Plan Your Visit:

    Timing: 10:30 AM to 6:30 PM

    Closed on Fridays and National Holidays

    Entry Fee: 15 Rupees for Adults and 10 Rupees for Childrens

    Foreign Nationals: 250 Rupees for adults and 125 Rupees for kids





  7. Jaipur City Palace Museum, Jaipur

    8. Located within the City Palace complex in Jaipur, this museum offers insights into the royal heritage of Rajasthan. The museum's exhibits include royal costumes, manuscripts, and armory, along with an impressive collection of Mughal and Rajasthani miniatures

    Museum type: Museum

    Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan

    Plan Your Visit

    Timing: 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM

    Entry Fee: 300 Rupees for Adults(above 12 years) and 150 Rupees for children(between 5 to 12 years) and 150 Rupees for senior citizen

    Foreign Nationals: 1000 Rupees for Adults(above 12 years) and 1000 Rupees for children(between 5 to 12 years)





  8. Calico Museum of Textiles, Ahmedabad

    9. Known for its comprehensive collection of Indian textiles, the Calico Museum showcases the rich textile heritage of India, featuring traditional fabrics, embroideries, and intricate patterns from different regions and periods

    Museum type: History museum, Textile museum

    Location: Opposite The Underbridge, Shahibag Ahmedabad Gujarat, India

    Plan Your Visit

    Timing: Morning(10:30 AM to 12:30 PM) and Afternoon(2:30 PM to 4:30 PM)

    Entry Fee: Free for all





  9. National Rail Museum, New Delhi

    10. This museum offers a fascinating look at India's railway heritage, with an extensive collection of historic locomotives, carriages, and railway artifacts. Interactive exhibits and a toy train ride make it a unique and engaging experience for visitors of all ages.

    Museum type: Rail museum

    Location: Chanakyapuri, New Delhi

    Plan Your Visit

    Timing: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM

    Entry Fee: 50 Rupees for Adults(above 12 years) in weekdays and 100 Rupees in weekend and 10 Rupees for Childrens(between 3-12 years) in weekdays and 20 Rupees in weekend





  10. Dr. Bhau Daji Lad Museum, Mumbai

    11. Located in Mumbai, this museum, formerly known as the Victoria and Albert Museum, showcases the city's cultural heritage and history through exhibits of fine and decorative arts, historical photographs, and models. Its beautifully restored interiors are a testament to its rich legacy.

    Museum type: Museum

    Location: Veer Mata Jijabai Bhonsle Udyan, Dr. Baba Saheb Ambedkar Marg, Byculla East, Mumbai, Maharashtra

    Plan Your Visit

    Timing: 10:00 AM to 5:30 PM

    Entry Fee: 10 Rupees for Adults(above 13 years) and 50 Rupees for Childrens(between 6-12 years)

    Foreign Nationals: 100 Rupees for Adults(above 13 years) and 50 Rupees for Childrens(between 6-12 years)
